# Decisions

2026-04-26 — Project created. Working title: "What Do We Mean by *Language*? A Pluralist Map for the *Language Sciences*." Reason: predecessor project (`Three_purposes_three_HPC_categories_of_language`, now archived) accumulated four rounds of review-board feedback that converged on a different paper than originally framed; the cleaner move was to start fresh with the consolidated reframe rather than rewrite an architecture that was no longer load-bearing.

2026-04-26 — Working thesis: pluralist map of recurrent sense-clusters for the word *language*, with HPC discipline (projectibility, stabilizers, slippage diagnostics) supplying the methodological backbone that Santana 2016's three-criteria pluralism lacks. Reason: separates the lexical/conceptual map from the realist-discipline contribution. Addresses the round 1-3 panel's convergence pressure that the predecessor's "field-relative HPC categories" framing was at risk of being read as a re-derivation of pluralism in HPC vocabulary.

2026-04-26 — Engaging Santana 2016 ("What Is Language?", *Ergo* 3.19) as the closest published precedent. The HPC contribution must do work Santana's three-criteria pluralism does not. Reason: the intake notes from the predecessor project flagged the structural overlap; honest engagement protects the paper from being read as Santana plus terminology.

2026-04-26 — Primary venue: *Language Sciences* (8000-10000 words). Reason: best fit for transdisciplinary metatheoretical work on how the object of language studies is constructed. Compass-style survey or *Theoretical Linguistics* target-article formats were considered and rejected for now; the survey form would underclaim the HPC contribution, and the target-article form would push the paper toward debate-driven structure that the cluster map doesn't naturally take.

2026-04-26 — Brainstorm cluster list (12 entries) needs consolidation. Likely landing zone is 5-7 clusters. Specific consolidation candidates: #1 (general human capacity) and #8 (cognitive/neural faculty) collapse to one cluster at different vocabularies; #2 (named sociohistorical object) and parts of #11 (index of identity / standardization) project together; #6 (communicative behaviour) and #7 (modality) may merge as use+instantiation. Reason: 8000-10000 words cannot support full HPC treatment of 12 clusters, and several of the 12 are at uneven grain.

2026-04-26 — Cluster consolidation locked at six: Capacity, Idiolect / I-language, Population-level convention / E-language, Named sociohistorical object, Abstract formal system, Communicative practice. Reason: clean coverage of the brainstorm pairs that merged on shared projection targets, while keeping individual-internal vs population-level vs institutional-object distinctions that working scientists treat as different.

2026-04-26 — Three open boundary cases resolved. **(#9) Broader semiotic system:** handled in §2 as the genus that the six clusters sit within, not a cluster. Animal communication and programming languages stay as boundary cases under Capacity and Communicative practice. **(#10) Language of thought:** set aside in §2 with a two-sentence note framing it as a metaphorical extension (the Fodor 1975 move ported language-like properties into mental representation) that doesn't share the projectibility profile of the public-object senses. Whether mental representation is language-like is a different debate. Not a cluster. **(#12) Methodological construct:** dropped as a candidate cluster ("which methods? which fields?" — too broad). Operationalisation-slippage ("the corpus IS the language", "competence IS the language") becomes one of the slippage patterns catalogued in the slippage section. Reason: keeps the paper at six clusters for the 8000-10000 word budget; absorbs the brainstorm material that wasn't doing distinct cluster-level work.

2026-04-26 — Predecessor archived at `archive/Three_purposes_three_HPC_categories_of_language/` with full git history, round 1-4 reviews, revised plan, and initial main.tex draft preserved.

2026-04-26 — Advisory board: carry over predecessor's panel with one swap. Drop Haspelmath (the comparative-concept umbrella question is settled — umbrella is a coordinative label, period). Add Santana (closest published precedent; can tell whether the paper does work his three-criteria pluralism doesn't). Six total: Boyd, Khalidi, Ereshefsky, Santana, Agha, Fitch. Personas updated to reflect the new paper's framing (cluster-by-cluster HPC discipline, slippage diagnoses, coordinative label for the umbrella) rather than the predecessor's field-relative-categories framing.

2026-04-26 — Source-grounding pass complete before drafting. Santana 2016 read in full; HPC book chapters 4 / 7 / 8 / 16 + Predictions appendix read; Casten 2026 quantitative claims (β = 0.18 on F1, N = 350 EpiSLI, replication N > 100,000, 49 vocal-learning species, 8 Neanderthals + 2 Denisovans, less than 0.1% of genome, 188× predictive power) verified against the primary source. Synthesis written to `notes/source-grounding-summary.md`. Reason: the paper's contribution is the synthesis of Santana's pluralism and Boyd's projectibility move into a more granular cluster taxonomy with HPC discipline; the prose has to engage Santana substantively (not paraphrase from intake notes) and the §1 hook depends on accurate Casten claims. Source-grounding before drafting protects both.

2026-04-26 — Capacity per-cluster template populated with five sub-axes (faculty/processing-architecture / broad-faculty / developmental-acquisition / comparative-biology/vocal-learning / impairment-phenotype) at `notes/cluster-template-capacity.md`. Reason: the source-grounded HCF/PJ/FHC/JP exchange shows that the Capacity cluster is fat in the HPC-book chapter 8 sense (one label, mechanistically distinct sub-axes with different projection targets). Treating Capacity as a single homogeneous cluster would obscure exactly the slippage discipline the paper supplies; the sub-axis structure makes the §1 Casten/Pinker hook a within-cluster sub-axis slippage (sub-axes 3 + 4 → sub-axis 1). The five sub-axes are not closed; if §3 drafting reveals that one sub-axis splits or two collapse, revisit.

2026-04-26 — Communicative practice per-cluster template populated at `notes/cluster-template-communicative-practice.md` with five sub-axes (speech-act / illocutionary force; interactional / situational organization; communicative competence / appropriateness per Hymes 1992; metapragmatic typification / register / enregistered voice per Agha 2005; modality cross-cutting). Reason: the four theoretical traditions (Searle, Goffman, Hymes, Agha) overlap on stabilizers more than the Capacity sub-axes did but project to distinct inferences, so within-cluster sub-axis discipline matters. The operationalisation-slippage worked examples ("the corpus IS the language" / "the LLM output IS the language" / "the treebank IS the language") are sketched here for forward reference to §9. Goffman 1967 and Searle 1969 primaries flagged as read-on-demand for §6 prose drafting.

2026-04-26 — All four remaining per-cluster templates populated: Idiolect/I-language at `notes/cluster-template-idiolect.md` (3 sub-axes); Population-level convention at `notes/cluster-template-population-convention.md` (3 sub-axes); Named sociohistorical object at `notes/cluster-template-named-sociohistorical.md` (3 sub-axes); Abstract formal system at `notes/cluster-template-abstract-formal.md` (4 sub-axes). Source-grounding for these drew on Devitt 2008 (read in full); Eckert 2008 indexical field (sampled); Silverstein 2003 indexical order (sampled); Pennycook 2010 *Language as a Local Practice* (sampled); Jäger & Rogers 2012 formal-language theory (sampled). Reason: each cluster's sub-axis structure was determined by where the established literature draws methodologically distinct projection targets — coordination convention vs speech-community variables vs stylistic indexicality for Population convention; institutional vs identity-marker vs descended-kind for Named sociohistorical object; formal-language theory vs formal semantics vs Platonism vs NLP for Abstract formal system; psychological-conception vs linguistic-conception vs empirical-variation for Idiolect/I-language. The HCF/PJ recursion-only debate is identified as a worked cross-cluster slippage example (Abstract-formal-system Sub-axis 1 → Capacity Sub-axis 1) that §9 can use alongside the Casten/Pinker case. Source-grounding gaps flagged in each template (Goffman/Searle for Communicative practice; Chomsky 1986/1995/2013 for Idiolect; Lewis 1969 / Labov 1972 primaries for Population convention; Haugen / Bourdieu / Makoni & Pennycook 2007 for Named sociohistorical object; Katz 1981/1984 / Montague primaries for Abstract formal system) — each gap is read-on-demand and doesn't block §-prose drafting.

Reason: simulated reports converged on those as the issues most likely to drive a serious revision request, so subsequent edits prioritized table transparency, operationalized perturbation evidence, case-specific warrant, and a broad/narrow faculty distinction.

2026-04-27 — Source-grounding fixes should prefer claim-softening over citation sprawl. The Casten/Wirthlin claim was softened from auditory-cortex temporal-processing selection to vocal-learning-related regulatory architecture; Table 1 dropped unsourced Hebrew/Serbo-Croatian examples; the Cantonese worked case was narrowed to HK census categories, Hong Kong education policy, and diaspora census channels with verified local bib entries. 2026-04-27 — Grammaticality should be framed as a recurrent HPC licensing regime or kind-schema, not casually as one global HPC kind. Reason: the language paper's pluralist map puts pressure on any extensional "one kind" claim; the compatible version is architectural unity across local conditioned licensing regimes.

Reason: the paper is genuinely cross-field; both repositories match different halves of the intended readership. Use author-manuscript metadata only and update later with DOI/citation rather than uploading publisher-formatted files.
