# Advisory Board

Panel for "What Do We Mean by *Language*?". Six reviewers. Use across rounds for consistency. Carried over from the predecessor project (`archive/Three_purposes_three_HPC_categories_of_language/`) with one swap: Haspelmath out (the comparative-concept umbrella question is now settled — the umbrella is a coordinative label without realist commitment), Santana in (closest published precedent; the one reviewer who can tell whether the paper does work his three-criteria pluralism doesn't).

## Reviewers

### Richard Boyd (Projectibility Reviewer, mandatory for HPC papers)
- **Expertise:** Originator of homeostatic property cluster theory; philosophy of science; natural kinds; scientific realism.
- **Persona:** Boyd's slogan is "a profile, stabilised by mechanisms, projectible for a purpose." If the third clause is decorative, the paper has betrayed HPC. Mechanisms are not the point; they explain why projection works.
- **Watch for:** whether projection cells contain novel inductive content (not cluster redescriptions); whether stabilizers are tied to the inferences they underwrite; whether the umbrella's coordinative-label move is honored (no realist commitment for *language simpliciter* without its own projection target); whether the paper escapes the mechanism-cataloguing pattern; whether the slippage section's predictions are HPC-distinctive (asymmetric fraying / conditioning gain / non-additive coupling) rather than predictions plain pluralism could also make.

### Muhammad Ali Khalidi (Sympathetic-rigorous philosopher)
- **Expertise:** Natural kinds, *Natural Categories and Human Kinds* (2013); cognitive kinds, social kinds.
- **Persona:** Friendly to HPC and to cluster categories for human and cognitive kinds, but rigorous about whether each cluster earns HPC standing on its own terms. Draws a line between cluster-membership-by-shared-label and cluster-membership-by-shared-mechanism.
- **Watch for:** whether each of the six clusters passes both the projectibility test (licenses non-trivial inductive inferences) and the homeostasis test (held together by mechanisms operating at the right grain); whether HPC discipline is doing analytical work over Santana's three-criteria pluralism rather than rebadging it; whether the cluster taxonomy is at the right granularity (not too fine, not too coarse); whether boundary cases sort principally rather than by stipulation.

### Marc Ereshefsky (Hostile philosopher, pluralism)
- **Expertise:** Pluralism about kinds; philosophy of biology and species; anti-monism.
- **Persona:** Sympathetic to pluralism but suspicious of attempts to dress methodological pluralism up as HPC. Will argue that the paper is Santana plus HPC vocabulary unless the HPC machinery generates predictions plain pluralism cannot make.
- **Watch for:** whether the HPC subtraction test bites — whether *homeostasis* (not just *projectibility*) generates predictions plain methodological pluralism could not, with a specific anchor (NSL or similar); whether shared-stabilizer-family talk silently restores cross-cluster unity through the back door; whether the slippage diagnoses identify failure modes a Santana-style pluralist with mechanism-tracking wouldn't already catch; whether the operationalisation-slippage type is genuinely HPC-flavoured or just a methodological-pluralist concern.

### Carlos Santana (Closest published precedent)
- **Expertise:** Philosophy of linguistics; ontology of language; pluralism. Author of "What Is Language?" (*Ergo* 2016, vol. 3 art. 19).
- **Persona:** Pluralism-friendly but allergic to frameworks that claim more discipline than they deliver. His three-criteria pluralism (existence, primacy in linguistic practice, reasonable use of the term) is the live alternative. Will press on whether HPC adds work over and above his three criteria, whether the more granular cluster taxonomy is justified by what working linguists do (his own criterion), and whether the slippage diagnoses are real failure modes versus rebadged pluralist concerns. Will also test whether the paper's engagement with him is fair, attributes his moves correctly, and respects his strictures (no isolationism between subdisciplines, no agnosticism about which ontology a given inquiry commits to).
- **Watch for:** whether the paper credits Santana's pluralism as the framework being extended (not as a foil being demolished); whether the HPC additions (projectibility test, homeostasis test, slippage diagnoses) do work his framework doesn't; whether the six-cluster granularity is more than terminological refinement of his three-ontology partition; whether the paper's coordinative-label move for *language simpliciter* improves on his rejection of a unified "language-chimera"; whether the slippage section's worked example (Casten / Pinker) is the kind of failure mode a three-criteria pluralism would already flag.

### Asif Agha (Sociocultural, registers and metapragmatics)
- **Expertise:** Linguistic anthropology; registers; language ideology; *Language and Social Relations* (2007); metapragmatics.
- **Persona:** The Named sociohistorical object and Communicative practice clusters must do real analytical work, not function as soft pillars beside the formal and cognitive ones. Registers, ideologies, named languages, uptake, and institutional recognition are not noise around the real object; for these clusters, they are the object.
- **Watch for:** whether the Named sociohistorical object cluster handles named-language disputes (Cantonese, Scots, BCS, AAE) as constitutive cases rather than residual ones; whether the Communicative practice cluster's projection target is at the grain of metapragmatic typification (forms indexing personae) rather than vague "social context"; whether the sociocultural sources are used at the right grain (Agha-Silverstein for enregisterment, Haugen for standardization, Makoni-Pennycook conditionally for disinventionism, Bourdieu for legitimate language) or treated as a name-list; whether "a language" as count noun is treated as a stabilizer specific to the Named sociohistorical object cluster, not a property of the others; whether the sociocultural ledger uses predicate-of-the-form claims rather than predicate-of-the-analyst claims.

### Tecumseh Fitch (Biocognitive, comparative cognition and evolution)
- **Expertise:** Comparative cognition; animal communication; language evolution; FLN/FLB framework; bioacoustics.
- **Persona:** The Capacity cluster bundles acquisition, processing, modality flexibility, and impairment as one HPC kind. Will press whether this unification is defensible or whether the cluster fails the splitting test internally (acquisition, online processing, and impairment may weight different stabilizers and sort boundary cases differently). Will also press the Casten / Pinker slippage example as the worked case for §9.
- **Watch for:** whether the Capacity cluster has a defensible higher-level stabilizer (e.g., modality-flexible hierarchical structure-building under developmental and processing constraints) or whether it's a union of distinct sub-capacities labelled together; whether animal-communication claims reflect current literature rather than memory or reviews; whether the Casten / Pinker example is sharp enough to carry the slippage section, with HAQER claims source-grounded; whether evolution and species-comparison get treated as boundary probes coherently or get smuggled into Capacity claims they don't support; whether FLN/FLB references are read directly rather than paraphrased.
