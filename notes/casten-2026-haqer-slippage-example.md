## Why this paper matters for `Three_purposes`

The paper is a clean live case of `LANGUAGE`-label slippage. The same word `LANGUAGE` attaches to four distinct things in the conversation around this paper:

1. **HAQER regulatory polygenic signal.** Less than 0.1% of the genome; mostly noncoding regulatory regions; β = 0.18 on the F1 factor in the EpiSLI cohort. Field of measurement: regulatory genomics.
2. **F1 verbal-performance composite.** Driven primarily by sentence repetition; draws on morphosyntax, phonology, working memory, attention, lexical knowledge, and task compliance. Field of measurement: developmental psycholinguistics / clinical assessment.
3. **Cross-mammalian vocal-learning toolkit.** HAQER-like sequences show convergent evolution across 49 vocal-learning mammals (cetaceans, bats, pinnipeds, songbird parallels). Field of measurement: comparative regulatory genomics.
4. **The Pinker-Bloom 1990 modular language faculty.** A Chomskyan grammatical capacity, structurally specified, distinct from general cognition. Field of operation: theoretical linguistics / philosophy of mind.

The paper measures (2). It gestures at (1) and (3). It is read in the popular reception (Pinker's tweet is the documented case) as evidence for (4).

Each of (1), (2), (3), (4) is a defensible field-relative `LANGUAGE` category if held to its own projection target and stabilizers. The slippage between them is what produces the overclaim. This is exactly the diagnosis the paper makes.

## Use as splitting-test material

The plan currently uses LLM output for grammatical `LANGUAGE`, signed language for biocognitive `LANGUAGE`, and Cantonese for sociocultural `LANGUAGE`. The Casten et al. paper supplies a different and arguably stronger live case for the biocognitive splitting test: it lets the paper show the slippage between sub-categories *within* biocognitive `LANGUAGE`, not just at the boundary with sociocultural and grammatical clusters.

Suggested split inside the biocognitive cluster, motivated by this paper:

- **(b1) Verbal-performance phenotypes** (the F1 composite). Projectible for clinical and developmental prediction. Stabilizers: developmental neurodevelopmental gain, working memory, attention, lexical retrieval, motor speech, articulation.
- **(b2) Vocal-learning regulatory architecture** (HAQERs and the cross-mammalian convergence). Projectible for cross-species comparison and developmental neurogenetics. Stabilizers: regulatory tuning of prenatal expression, transcription factor binding, chromatin accessibility patterns.
- **(b3) Language faculty in the modular sense** (recursive hierarchical syntax, displacement, the LAD). Projectible for structural acquisition predictions. Stabilizers (if any): contested; classical Chomskyan view posits species-specific genetic endowment, but the present paper does not in fact provide evidence for this.

The Pinker reception conflates (b1), (b2), and (b3). The paper's own findings, read carefully, support (b1) and (b2) and are silent on (b3). That is the splitting test landing in the wild.

## Methodological caveats to carry into the paper

If `Three_purposes` cites Casten et al. as a worked example, carry the methodological caveats so the citation does not over-borrow:

- Discovery N = 350 in EpiSLI with multiple factors, annotations, PGS partitions, and matched controls.
- CP-PGS weights derived from a cognitive performance GWAS that is entangled with education, cognition, and SES before HAQER stratification. UK Biobank follow-up uses a word-reading PGS as a partial corrective.
- F1 construct broader than "language" (see (b1) above).
- Replications phenotypically coarser: SPARK is autism-enriched; ABCD uses verbal learning; UK Biobank uses verbal working memory and educational attainment.
- Archaic-genome N = 10 (8 Neanderthals, 2 Denisovans) underwrites "Neanderthals had language" framing in the paper's reception, which is much weaker than the press version implies.
- Canonical correlation optimized over variables underwriting the obstetric-dilemma story.

Citing the paper as supporting *the existence of label slippage between (b1), (b2), and (b3)* does not depend on the magnitude of these effect sizes; it depends on what was measured. So the caveats matter less for `Three_purposes`'s use of the paper than for `echolocation`'s use of it. Still worth flagging to keep the prose honest.
